What stands out

Four things worth knowing before treating 83.6% as a single number.

96.2% accuracy on 26 pitches

3–0 is the model's best call

Every 3–0 pitch in this group averages 92.6% confidence and is right all but one time. This is the closest thing to a sure thing in the whole dataset — and the least surprising, since 3–0 fastballs are a league-wide near-automatic.

34% of the group is 0–0

Confidence with the least information

85 of 250 pitches are the first pitch of a plate appearance — prev_1_pitch_class = NONE, no sequencing signal at all. The model is still 85.9% accurate here, but it's leaning almost entirely on Gausman's overall FF/FS split, not situational read.

8 of the 12 highest-confidence misses

Whiff-on-splitter, expects another

A recurring miss pattern: previous pitch was FS / swinging_strike, model calls FS again at 75–84% confidence — and Gausman comes back with the fastball instead. This single pattern accounts for roughly a fifth of all 41 misses.

50% accuracy on 2–0 (n=8)

A confident soft spot

Small sample, but notable: 2–0 counts sit at 79.4% mean confidence in this bucket — comparable to 1–0 or 2–1 — yet land at just 50% accuracy, the worst of any count state here.
